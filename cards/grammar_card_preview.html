English · grammar structure
He might have gone out.
It is possible that he went out, but the speaker is not sure.
Structure
might have + past participle
How it works
might — shows uncertainty or possibility
have gone — refers to a completed action in the past
When to use it
Use this structure when making a guess about something that may have happened earlier.
Natural context
He is not answering his phone. He might have gone out.
Contrast
He might go out. — A possibility now or in the future.
He might have gone out. — A possibility about the past.
Common mistakes
He might has gone out. → He might have gone out.
